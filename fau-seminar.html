FAU Seminar — Piezoelectric Composite Homogenization

Computing effective electromechanical properties from micro-models with periodic boundary conditions.

Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) · Seminar project

What this work is about

Many devices use piezoelectric polymer–ceramic composites where a stiff, active inclusion sits in a soft polymer matrix. Modeling a full product with every particle/fiber is impractical, so we compute a single homogenized (effective) property matrix from a small periodic unit cell (RVE) and use it at component scale.

  • Built RVE models in Abaqus and applied periodic boundary conditions.
  • Varied matrix stiffness (PDMS soft ↔ stiff), inclusion type (particle/fiber), and volume fraction (5%/10%).
  • Ran unit mechanical/electrical load cases, volume-averaged fields, and assembled the effective property matrix.

Results at a glance

Observed trends

  • Matrix stiffness matters: a stiffer matrix reduces overall deformation under mechanical loading and moderates electrical-to-mechanical coupling.
  • Inclusion topology: fiber inclusions generally show stronger through-thickness response and coupling than isolated particles at the same volume fraction.
  • Volume fraction: moving from 5% → 10% inclusion raises the effective stiffness and coupling magnitudes in the homogenized matrix.
  • Directional effects: periodic arrangements create banded response patterns; the homogenized properties (block matrix above) capture this in Voigt form for component-scale FE use.

What this enables

The workflow converts detailed microstructure behavior into a compact effective property matrix suitable for fast component simulations. Designers can screen polymer stiffness, inclusion type, and volume fraction without re-meshing full assemblies—useful for early design and optimization studies.

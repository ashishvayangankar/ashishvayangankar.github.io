2-D Pantograph — Nonlinear FE Study (Abaqus)

Carried out nonlinear finite-element analysis in Abaqus to assess the load-bearing response of a simplified two-dimensional pantograph mechanism.

How I did it (short)

  • Built a planar FE model: 2-D beam/truss links with realistic elastic properties; hinge joints modeled via connector/MPC constraints; one node grounded to remove rigid-body motion.
  • Nonlinear static step: enabled NLGEOM for large rotations; displacement-controlled actuation at the end effector; optional hard stops/contact to prevent link overlap; automatic stabilization for robust convergence.
  • Response extraction: computed reaction forces and kinematics to get the load–displacement curve, effective stiffness, and stability limits; ran quick parametric sweeps on link lengths and joint stiffness to study sensitivity.

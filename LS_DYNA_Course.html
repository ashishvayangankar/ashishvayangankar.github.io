LS-DYNA Crash & Explicit Simulations — Online Course

Hands-on impact, crash, vibration and post-processing workflows (2025)

What I worked on

  • Performed impact analysis on honeycomb and stacked composite sandwich panels.
  • Modeled displacement-controlled crash box crush to study folding and energy absorption.
  • Ran modal and FRF-based vibration analyses on representative structural models.
  • Used LS-PrePost for meshing, contacts, plots, animations, and results reporting.

Impact analysis on a composite sandwich structure

Explicit impact simulation on a sandwich panel with a honeycomb core, sandwiched between composite face sheets with 0°/90°/±45° stacking. Used to study local damage, energy absorption and contact forces under low-velocity impact.

Crash box crush analysis

Displacement-controlled loading where a rigid plate pushes into the front bumper, crushing the thin-walled crash box. Used to visualize progressive folding, energy absorption and reaction forces.

Vibration and FRF analysis

  • Computed natural frequencies and mode shapes of representative structural components.
  • Post-processed time histories to obtain FRF curves at selected nodes and directions.
  • Checked mode participation, damping assumptions and peak responses against expectations from theory.

Typical LS-DYNA workflow

  • Pre-processing in LS-PrePost: geometry import, meshing, material and section assignment.
  • Definition of contact, boundary conditions and loading for impact, crash and vibration cases.
  • Solution control: timestep monitoring, mass scaling checks and output requests.
  • Post-processing: stress/strain contours, history plots, energy checks and export of GIFs and figures.

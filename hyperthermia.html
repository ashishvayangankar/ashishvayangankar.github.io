Nanoparticle-Aided Hyperthermia

COMSOL multiphysics modelling for liver-tumor ablation

2021 · BMS College of Engineering

Published article: IJISRT (Vol. 6, Issue 12, 2021)

What I did

  • Built a coupled electromagnetics–bioheat model in COMSOL to study microwave hyperthermia around a coaxial slot antenna.
  • Modelled tissue heating with the Pennes bioheat equation and thermal damage using an Arrhenius-type rate; compared baseline hyperthermia with a nanoparticle-enhanced case.
  • Parameterized inputs (power, exposure time) and quantified thermal coverage and time-to-necrosis thresholds near clinically relevant limits.

Key findings (short)

  • Nanoparticles improved the heating rate and produced a more uniform temperature field in the tumor region.
  • Baseline (no nanoparticles) required significantly longer exposure to reach ablative temperatures; nanoparticle-aided runs reduced that time substantially.
  • Isotherm maps confirmed smoother radial gradients with nanoparticles, lowering the risk of over-hot spots near the applicator.

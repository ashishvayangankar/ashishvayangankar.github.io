Rockfall Protection Net — FAU

Particle–spring (Kelvin–Voigt) model using Python, validated contact, and boulder drop response.

What I built

  • Net as a discretized grid: square lattice of point masses connected by spring–damper elements (Kelvin–Voigt); edges clamped.
  • Contact model: normal contact between the boulder and net nodes via a Kelvin–Voigt law; benchmarked with simple sphere–sphere/plane cases.
  • Scenarios: static sag under gravity and a boulder drop to study deformation, arrest, and settling to equilibrium.

Key outcomes

  • The net captures and supports the boulder, then oscillates and settles to a stable equilibrium.
  • Stiffness and damping in the elements govern peak deflection and rebound—higher stiffness reduces sag, while damping controls oscillations.
  • Model extensions that improve realism: nonlinear spring behavior, shear links, frictional contact, pretension, and aerodynamic drag.
